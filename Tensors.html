Introduction to Tensors and Tensor Algebra

What is a Tensor?

A tensor is a mathematical object that generalizes scalars, vectors, and matrices to higher dimensions. Tensors are used extensively in physics, engineering, and machine learning.

  • Scalar: A single number (rank 0 tensor), e.g., temperature at a point.
  • Vector: A set of numbers (rank 1 tensor), e.g., velocity \( \mathbf{v} = (v_x, v_y, v_z) \).
  • Matrix: A two-dimensional array (rank 2 tensor), e.g., stress or strain in a material.
  • Higher-order tensors: Extend beyond matrices to describe more complex relationships.

Tensor Notation and Indexing

A tensor can be represented as:

\[ T^i_j \]

where:

  • The upper index \( i \) denotes a contravariant component.
  • The lower index \( j \) denotes a covariant component.

A second-order tensor example:

\[ T_{ij} = \begin{bmatrix} T_{11} & T_{12} \\ T_{21} & T_{22} \end{bmatrix} \]

Tensor Operations

Addition and Subtraction

\[ A_{ij} + B_{ij} = C_{ij} \]

Scalar Multiplication

\[ (\lambda T)_{ij} = \lambda T_{ij} \]

Tensor Product

\[ (A \otimes B)_{ijkl} = A_{ij} B_{kl} \]

Contraction (Index Summation)

\[ C_i = A_{ij} B^j \]
